# Modes

**NOSTALGIA** has no discrete mode switch — its character is shaped continuously by the interaction of the two feedback paths and the LFO.

## Single-Loop Echo (FEEDBACK 1 only)

With FEEDBACK 2 at zero and FEEDBACK 1 raised, only the first two stages regenerate. This produces a warmer, slightly filtered echo that softens with each repeat. Because signal passes through all three stages before exiting, the dry signal still gains the full three-stage coloration, but the repeats are generated within the shorter sub-chain.

## Full-Chain Regeneration (FEEDBACK 2 only)

With FEEDBACK 1 at zero and FEEDBACK 2 raised, the full three-stage delay loops back on itself. Each repeat passes through all three stages again, accumulating bucket-brigade coloration and high-frequency roll-off. At high settings this creates long, deteriorating tail sequences. At maximum it self-oscillates into a sustained tone.

## Dual Feedback (both FEEDBACK controls raised)

Combining both feedback paths creates complex regeneration patterns — the early stages develop their own sub-echoes while the outer loop sustains the full chain. This is the most musically dense setting and the most sensitive to the TIME knob position.

## Chorus / Vibrato (LFO engaged)

Raising LFO DEPTH with a short TIME setting (TIME knob at roughly 7–9 o'clock) turns **NOSTALGIA** into a chorus or vibrato effect. The internal LFO continuously sweeps delay time, producing pitch modulation proportional to LFO DEPTH and RATE. No external modulation source is required.

## External Signal Processing (EXT IN)

Plugging a non-modular source into EXT IN instead of IN routes the padded signal through the same three-stage chain. All controls, CV, and feedback behavior remain identical. This mode does not modify the circuit — it is purely an input level difference.
