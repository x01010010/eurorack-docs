# Overview

**NOSTALGIA** is a 3-stage hybrid delay module from Dreadbox, rooted in the echo circuit of the Erebus desktop synthesizer. Three bucket-brigade delay stages are wired in series, each contributing its own coloration to produce dense, evolving echoes that feel distinctly analog in character.

The delay time is voltage-controlled across a wide range — from tight slapback at 30 ms up to cavernous 1-second repeats — and a built-in LFO can continuously sweep the time for chorus, vibrato, and tape-flutter effects without any external modulation source. Two independent feedback paths give the module a split personality: one loop feeds the early stages for short, tight regeneration, and the other wraps around the full three-stage chain for long, progressively degrading tails.

Unusually for a Eurorack delay, **NOSTALGIA** provides outputs for the first and second delay taps as well as the final output, enabling stereo spread, parallel processing, and rhythmic offset tricks. A dedicated EXT input with instrument-level padding means the module accepts guitar or keyboard directly alongside modular-level signals.
