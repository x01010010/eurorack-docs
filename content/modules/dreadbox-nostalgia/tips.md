# Tips & Tricks

## Use TAP 1 OUT as a Wet/Dry Blend Point

TAP 1 OUT is quieter than the main OUT (approximately −6 dB). Rather than treating this as a limitation, use it as a pre-tamed wet signal that sits underneath an unprocessed dry signal in a mixer without overwhelming it.

## Pair FEEDBACK 1 and FEEDBACK 2 for Textural Control

FEEDBACK 1 thickens the early part of the decay. FEEDBACK 2 sustains the full tail. Starting with only FEEDBACK 1 raised gives you a warm, self-contained echo cluster; then slowly raising FEEDBACK 2 introduces a long smeared tail behind it. The two controls interact and reward slow, deliberate adjustment.

## Short TIME + High FEEDBACK 2 = Karplus–Strong-Style Plucks

Set TIME very short (fully counterclockwise) and bring FEEDBACK 2 up high. Trigger a short burst of noise or a sharp transient into IN. The three stages cycling at short times will pitch up the resonance into a plucked-string-like tone. Adjust TIME for pitch.

## Let the LFO Handle Modulation So You Don't Have To

In smaller patches where every modulation source is occupied, **NOSTALGIA**'s built-in LFO frees up your LFO module for other duties. Even a small amount of LFO DEPTH brings the delay to life and prevents it from sounding static or clinical.

## Self-Oscillation as a Sound Source

**NOSTALGIA** can function as a rudimentary pitched oscillator when FEEDBACK 2 is pushed into self-oscillation with no audio input. The pitch is coarse and the waveform is harmonically rich. Pair with a filter for a surprisingly usable lo-fi tone source.

## Protect Your Ears Before Pushing Feedback

Bring FEEDBACK 2 up slowly, especially at long TIME settings. The buildup can be sudden and loud. A VCA with an envelope patched to the signal chain downstream is good insurance during experimentation.
