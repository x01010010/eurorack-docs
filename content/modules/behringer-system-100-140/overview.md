# Overview

**140** is the modulation utility module from Behringer's System 100M reissue range — two independent **ADSR envelope generators** sharing a panel with one **analog LFO**. Every modular needs envelopes; the 140 packs two of them plus a delay-able LFO into 16 HP at a price below most single-channel envelopes.

## What you get

- **Two independent ADSR envelopes**, each with sliders for Attack, Decay, Sustain, and Release
- **Manual gate button** per envelope, plus an external gate input
- **Two positive outputs and one inverted output** per envelope
- **One LFO** with five selectable waveshapes (sine, triangle, pulse, sawtooth, reverse sawtooth)
- **Three frequency ranges** (low / mid / high) covering 0.05 Hz to 30 Hz
- **Delay slider** that gates the LFO off until N seconds after a trigger arrives
- **External trigger input** for phase-locking the LFO to keyboard or sequencer
- **Two LFO outputs** with a switch for full-level (10 V p-p) or 1/10 (1 V p-p) operation; lower output is inverted

## Why it stands out

Most Eurorack envelope generators are voltage-controlled. The 140 is **not** — there is no CV control over Attack, Decay, Sustain, or Release. What you get instead is two of them, plus an LFO, in 16 HP at a budget price, with the snappy analog character of the original 100M.

It's the right module when you need reliable utility envelopes for a kick drum, a bass voice, and a slow filter sweep, and don't need to modulate the envelope shapes themselves.

## What it sounds like

Snappy. The analog envelopes are punchy at fast settings — perfect for percussion — and smooth at long settings for pads. The LFO is clean and predictable, with a usable delay function that the original 100M was known for.
