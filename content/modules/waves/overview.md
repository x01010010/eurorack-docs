# Overview

**WAVES** is a 14 HP digital function generator and tidal modulator inspired by the second-generation Mutable Instruments Tides. At its core is an asymmetric triangle generator paired with a wavetable waveshaper, a two-pole low-pass filter, and a wavefolder — all of which together produce continuously morphing curves you can use as envelopes, LFOs, or audio-rate oscillators.

Where most envelope or LFO modules give you one signal, WAVES gives you **four related outputs** at once. The relationships between those outputs — phase offset, frequency ratio, or shape crossfade — are themselves voltage-controllable, which is what makes it especially useful as a polyrhythmic modulation source for filters, VCAs, panners, and effects.

## What It Is, Depending On How You Patch It

| Mode | Behaves Like |
| --- | --- |
| **AD** envelope | One-shot attack/decay envelope, 0 V to +8 V |
| **Cyclic** | Bipolar LFO or audio-rate digital oscillator, ±5 V |
| **ASR** envelope | Attack–sustain–release envelope held by gate length |

Combined with three frequency ranges (slow, medium, audio) and a switchable PLL clocking mode, WAVES covers everything from minute-long evolving modulation to harmonically rich oscillator tones.

## Why It's Different

- **Four simultaneous outputs** with voltage-controllable relationships.
- **Shape control** smoothly morphs between linear, exponential, and sinusoidal segments — far more expressive than a single curve knob.
- **Smoothness** is a single knob that runs from low-pass filtering on one side to wavefolding on the other.
- **PLL / clock sync** locks the oscillator to an external clock or audio signal, generating sub- and super-harmonics.
- **Per-CV attenuverters** on every modulation input. No need for an external utility module to tame CV depths.

## At a Glance

- **14 HP** Eurorack module
- **5 knobs + 5 attenuverters + 3 buttons**
- **8 CV inputs** (V/Oct, FM with attenuverter, Slope, Frequency, Smoothness, Shape, Shift/Level, Trig, Clock)
- **4 DC-coupled outputs** at 1 kΩ
- **16-bit CV resolution**
- **Power**: 50 mA (+12 V) / 20 mA (-12 V)
