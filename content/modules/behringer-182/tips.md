# Tips & Tricks

- **RESET for song structure** — patch a long-period gate (every 8 or 16 bars from a master sequencer) to RESET. The 182 restarts its sequence in sync with your song's phrase length, keeping patterns from drifting over time.
- **DELAY for polyrhythm** — the DELAY knob offsets channel 2's timing against channel 1. Even small amounts of delay create rhythmic displacement that adds groove. Maximum DELAY produces near-canon-style effects.
- **Random mode with low step count** — set MODE to Random and STEP NUMBER to 3 or 4. The sequencer cycles randomly through a small pool of pitches, creating a limited but unpredictable melodic cell.
- **GATE output to S&H** — patch GATE to a sample-and-hold trigger and CV1 to the S&H signal input. Each step samples itself — useful when you want to add slew or quantisation downstream of the 182.
- **Series mode as variation** — use SERIES mode and set the first 8 steps as a main phrase and the second 8 as a variation. The 16-step loop plays the main phrase, then the variation, then repeats — a simple but effective A/B structure.
- **Tune step knobs with a tuner** — use a chromatic tuner on the CV1 output and tune each step knob while holding the step at that position (use RESET + STOP). This ensures accurate pitches without guessing by ear.
