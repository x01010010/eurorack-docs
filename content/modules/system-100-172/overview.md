# Overview

**172** is the strangest and most useful module in Behringer's System 100M reissue range. It crams **four separate utilities** into 16 HP:

- A **6-stage analog phase shifter** with resonance and external-CV mod
- A short **analog BBD (bucket-brigade) delay** with feedback
- A simple **triangle LFO** with normal and inverted outputs
- A **gate delay / pulse shaper** with threshold and gate-time controls

Each section can run independently, and the LFO normalizes into the phase shifter and delay CV inputs when nothing is patched — so by default you have a self-modulating phaser/delay with no patching at all.

## What it sounds like

- The **phase shifter** is the warm, syrupy 6-stage type — closer to a Mu-Tron BiPhase than a stompbox phaser. With resonance up it gets vocal and almost wah-like.
- The **BBD delay** is short (1 ms to ~7 ms) — too short for echoes but **perfect for chorus, flanging, doubling, and Karplus-Strong-style plucks** when fed back to itself.
- The **LFO** is a clean triangle, slow (0.02–10 Hz), with an inverted output for stereo modulation.
- The **gate delay** turns a short trigger into a longer gate, or an early gate into a delayed one — a small but lifesaving utility for timing problems.

## Why pick it

The 172 is the module you didn't know you needed. It solves four common patch problems — modulation, doubling/chorus, slow LFO, gate timing — in one 16 HP slice. If you're building a small skiff, it might be the most flexible utility module Behringer makes.
