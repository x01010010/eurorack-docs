# Modes

The 172 has no operating modes — its four sections are independent and always active when their EFFECT ON/OFF switch is on. The "modes" are the patching configurations the panel encourages.

## Self-modulating phaser

The default. Engage the phaser EFFECT switch with no cables in the phaser CV IN. The internal LFO modulates SHIFT FREQ at the LFO FREQUENCY rate. Set RESONANCE to taste — at higher settings, the phaser becomes vocal and almost wah-like.

## Self-modulating chorus / flanger

Engage the delay EFFECT switch with no cables in the delay CV IN. Internal LFO modulates DELAY TIME. Mix SIG OUT with the dry source externally — about 50/50 for chorus, slightly wetter for flange. Increase RESONANCE for metallic flange tones.

## External modulation

Patch any CV (envelope, sequencer step, second LFO, S&H) into either CV IN to break the internal LFO normal. The MOD INTENSITY knob attenuates the external CV.

## Gate delay as pulse stretcher

Short trigger in, long gate out. Set GATE TIME high, leave DELAY TIME low. A short trigger pulse becomes a sustained gate — useful for triggering envelopes from short clock pulses.

## Gate delay as polyrhythm generator

Patch a clock into GATE IN, set DELAY TIME to a musical interval (e.g. half the clock period). The output gate fires offset from the clock — instant syncopation.

## LFO as standalone slow modulator

The LFO outputs are exposed even when neither effect is engaged. Use the 172 as a slow LFO + delayed-gate utility, with the phaser/delay sections as bonus features for when you need them.

## Karplus-Strong pluck

Engage the delay with very short DELAY TIME and high RESONANCE. Feed the SIG IN with a short noise burst (a noise source through a fast envelope) and the BBD line will resonate at a pitch determined by the delay time — a primitive Karplus-Strong string synthesis.
