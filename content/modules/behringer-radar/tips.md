# Tips & Tricks

- **Contact mic placement matters more than settings** — on a drum, centre of skin vs. rim produces very different audio characters. On a guitar body, near the bridge vs. near the nut captures different resonances. Experiment with placement before adjusting GAIN.
- **Double-sided tape for secure attachment** — foam mounting tape keeps the contact mic firmly in contact with the surface without mechanically loading the resonance. Avoid rigid mounts that damp vibration.
- **SENSITIVITY determines false trigger rate** — if GATE OUT is firing when you don't want it (vibration from nearby modules, handling noise), reduce SENSITIVITY. If it's missing quiet hits, increase it. The right setting is just above the noise floor of your environment.
- **ENV OUT for dynamic compression** — patch ENV OUT (inverted via an attenuverter at near-noon, turned CCW to invert) to a VCA CV input. Loud hits reduce the VCA level slightly — a basic dynamic levelling compressor from envelope follower + VCA.
- **Fast attack + slow decay for sustain pedal behaviour** — each tap gates a held CV that sustains long after the physical hit. Works well for driving a reverb mix or pad filter that should stay "open" between hits.
- **Low power draw for small cases** — at 10/5 mA, Radar is negligible on the PSU budget. Good choice for adding acoustic interfacing to a small skiff without worrying about headroom.
