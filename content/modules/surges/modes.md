# Modes

## Filter Slope (SLOPE switch)

| Setting | LP Slope | Character |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 2-pole | 12 dB/oct | Gentler rolloff, more open sound, more of the original character remains |
| 4-pole | 24 dB/oct | Steep rolloff, strong filtering, classic "thick" analogue filter sound |

BP and HP outputs are always 2-pole regardless of the SLOPE switch setting.

## Low-Pass Mode

LP output attenuates frequencies above the cutoff. At 4-pole, this is the classic Moog-style thick bass filter. At 2-pole it sounds more like a warm shelf. Resonance adds a peak at the cutoff; at maximum the filter self-oscillates.

## Band-Pass Mode

BP output passes only a band of frequencies around the cutoff, attenuating both low and high frequencies. Resonance tightens the bandwidth. Useful for formant-like filtering, wah effects, and isolating a specific frequency region.

## High-Pass Mode

HP output attenuates frequencies below the cutoff. Useful for removing low-frequency content, creating thin, bright textures, or isolating high-frequency content.

## Self-Oscillation

At maximum RESONANCE in LP mode, the filter oscillates, producing a sine wave at the cutoff frequency. The oscillation tracks V/OCT accurately enough for melodic use. The sine is available at LP output; use it as a sine oscillator or for FM/PM patching.

## Keyboard Tracking (V/OCT)

Patch a pitch CV to V/OCT. The filter cutoff tracks the pitch, keeping the timbre consistent as notes change pitch. At 100% tracking the cutoff rises and falls by 1 octave per volt — matching oscillator pitch tracking. Partial tracking (via an attenuverter before V/OCT) produces interesting timbral shifts with pitch.
