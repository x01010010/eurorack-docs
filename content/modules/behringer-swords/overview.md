# Overview

**SWORDS** is Behringer's reproduction of the **Mutable Instruments Blades** — a dual analog multi-mode filter built around the V2164 quad VCA chip. Same chip Émilie Gillet specified in the Blades schematic, same architecture, near-identical sonic identity, at roughly a quarter of the original price.

It is two 12 dB state-variable filters in 18 HP, with continuously morphable response (low → band → high pass per filter), an input drive stage that ranges from soft clipping to two-stage wavefolding, and continuously variable routing — single, parallel, or series — under both manual and CV control.

## What you get

- **Two 12 dB state-variable filters** with smooth, musical resonance
- **MODE control per filter** — continuously morphs low-pass → band-pass → high-pass
- **Drive per filter** with adjustable response from soft clipping to wavefolding
- **Self-oscillation** at high resonance, with low-frequency compensation that keeps the oscillation stable down low
- **Continuous routing** — single filter, parallel, or series — set by knob and modulatable via CV
- **SHIFT button** to couple the two cutoffs (filter 2 frequency knob becomes a transpose offset relative to filter 1)
- **V/oct input per filter** — accurate 1 V/oct tracking when self-oscillating, so it doubles as a stereo sine VCO
- **Attenuverters** on resonance, frequency, and routing CV

## Why it stands out

Most Behringer filters clone a single classic — Moog ladder, MS-20 Sallen-Key, Roland 24 dB. SWORDS clones an entirely modern design that was already a community favorite for a reason: dual filters with morphable mode and routing give you stereo processing, parallel band-splitting, and series 24 dB cascades from the same module.

The wavefolder built into the drive stage is the secret weapon. At low drive it's a clean overdrive; at high drive with the response control turned up, it folds the input into a complex harmonic source that the filter then sculpts. You can use it as a filter, as a wavefolder, as a stereo VCO (when self-oscillating), or all three at once.

## What it sounds like

Smooth, rich, never harsh. The 12 dB slopes are gentler than a Moog ladder — more open, more "voicey" in band-pass, more present in high-pass. The wavefolder adds the buzz and bite when you want it. Resonance sings at every cutoff frequency.
