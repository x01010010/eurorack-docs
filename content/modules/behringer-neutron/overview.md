# Overview

**NEUTRON** is Behringer's paraphonic semi-modular synthesizer, built around two CEM3340-derived VCOs and a dense patchbay offering 32 inputs and 24 outputs. Like all semi-modulars it ships internally normalled — plug in a MIDI cable and play immediately — but patching cables reconfigures any section of the signal chain.

The dual oscillators blend across five waveforms (tone mod, sawtooth, pulse, triangle, sine) with independent pulse-width control and can be played paraphonically: each oscillator responds to a different MIDI note simultaneously, enabling two-note chords or intervallic playing without an external sequencer.

Filtering is handled by a self-oscillating 12 dB/oct multimode VCF with simultaneous LP and HP outputs on dedicated jacks. Two independent ADSR generators handle filter and amplitude envelopes separately. An overdrive circuit sits in the signal path for analog saturation.

Beyond the core voice, the Neutron adds a multi-stage BBD analog delay, a flexible LFO with five shapes and MIDI clock sync, a patchable sample-and-hold with glide, a noise generator, and utility sections including attenuators, multiples, slew, and summers. MIDI and USB connectivity round it out.

The Neutron was designed in the UK and carries a notably higher power draw than most semi-modulars — budget accordingly.
