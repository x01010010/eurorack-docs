# Tips & Tricks

## Use it as four utilities, not one effect

The 172 is most useful when you stop thinking of it as a phaser and start thinking of it as four small modules sharing a panel. The LFO and gate delay alone justify the HP for most racks — the phaser and delay are bonuses.

## Self-oscillating phaser as sine source

With RESONANCE maxed and no input signal, the phaser self-oscillates as a nearly-pure sine wave. Modulate SHIFT FREQ with V/oct (through an attenuator) and you have a passable sine VCO with no extra modules.

## BBD delay is for chorus, not echo

7 ms is the maximum delay time — too short for echoes. It's perfect for chorus, flanger, doubling, and Karplus-Strong patches. Don't try to use it as a tape echo.

## The LFO normals are the killer feature

The internal LFO is automatically routed to both CV IN jacks. You can dial in a working phaser/delay sound without patching a single cable. Add an external CV only when you specifically want to break the normalization.

## Gate delay as syncopation

Patch your master clock into GATE IN, set DELAY TIME to ~50% of the clock period, take GATE OUT to a snare or hi-hat. Instant offbeat hits without programming a sequencer.

## Mix delay wet/dry externally

The delay output is wet-only. Patch the dry source and the delay's SIG OUT to a small mixer at roughly equal levels for chorus, or weight the wet higher for flange. A simple two-channel attenuator works.

## Pair with WAVES for envelope-following

Patch a WAVES envelope into PHASE CV IN to create a single-shot phaser sweep on each note. With AD shape and short timings, you get a per-note phase "pluck" rather than a continuous wash.

## Karplus-Strong stringer

The combination of short delay time, high resonance, and a noise burst into the delay input produces a string-like pluck. Modulate DELAY TIME with V/oct (attenuated) to play melodies. Crude, but distinctive.
