# Tips & Tricks

- **Watch the clip LED** — with three summing SIG IN jacks it's easy to drive the input hot. If the clip LED lights, back off the SIG IN knobs. The HIGH OUT can handle +18 dBu but your downstream module probably can't.
- **INITIAL GAIN as a manual VCA** — set INITIAL GAIN at a useful level and don't patch any CV. The channel becomes a static-gain amplifier or attenuator. Useful for mixing duties when you don't need dynamic control.
- **Exponential for voices, linear for everything else** — exponential is the right default for any patch where an envelope shapes amplitude. Switch to linear when you're doing controlled CV mixing, AM effects, or panning.
- **Use the LOW OUT for external gear** — the HIGH OUT can feed line-level mixers and audio interfaces directly. The LOW OUT is better matched to guitar pedals, amplifiers, and instrument-level inputs.
- **Stack MOD INs for layered modulation** — use all three MOD IN jacks simultaneously. One could be a slow envelope for overall loudness, one an LFO for tremolo, and one a fast envelope for transient punch. The knobs blend their contributions.
- **Summed outputs for master bus** — route the outputs of multiple sources into both VCA channels and take the summed HIGH OUT as your main mix. The 130 becomes a compact two-channel master mixer with CV-controlled levels.
- **Pair with the Black EG2** — the Black EG2's two envelope outputs pair naturally with the 130's two channels for a complete dual-voice amplitude section.
