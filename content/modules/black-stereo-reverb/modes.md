# Modes

## Reverb Sources

The SOURCE control selects the type of delay line used to build the reverb:

| Source | Character |
|---|---|
| **Tape** | Warm, slightly saturated, vintage character. Subtle high-frequency roll-off gives a natural, organic tail. |
| **BBD** | Bucket-brigade device emulation — slightly coloured with mild chorus-like artefacts. Smooth and musical. |
| **Dirty BBD** | BBD emulation with added noise and non-linearity. Grittier texture, lo-fi character while retaining spatial depth. |

## Room Modes

Each source is available in three room configurations. SIZE control fine-tunes the space within the selected mode:

| Mode | Character |
|---|---|
| **Room** | Small to medium spaces — natural ambience, short tail. Suitable for drums, rhythmic elements, and subtle spatial placement. |
| **Hall** | Larger concert hall spaces — longer decay, smoother tail. Suitable for melodic and harmonic content. |
| **Cathedral** | Very large reverberant spaces — long, expansive tail with high density. Suited to pads, drones, and ambient textures. |

## Freeze Mode

Holding the FREEZE button (or raising the FREEZE CV gate high) captures the current reverb buffer and loops it infinitely, ignoring new input. The frozen tail sustains as a drone or pad until FREEZE is released. The dry signal continues to pass through during Freeze.

## Preset Mode

Up to 10 full snapshots of all parameter states (source, mode, size, feedback, tone, spin, dry/wet) can be saved and recalled. The PRESET CV input steps through presets under voltage control, enabling preset switching from a sequencer or CV source during a performance.
