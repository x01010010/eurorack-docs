# Modes

## Ring Modulator

The ring modulator multiplies two input signals (X × Y), producing sum and difference sidebands. With audio in X and audio in Y, the output contains no original frequencies — only their sums and differences. With a slow CV in X and audio in Y, it acts as a VCA-like tremolo/AM modulator.

## Noise

White noise contains equal energy per frequency (flat spectrum). Pink noise rolls off at 3 dB/octave, giving it a warmer, more natural quality. Having two independent outputs per colour makes it easy to send different noise sources to multiple destinations (e.g., one to S&H input, one to a filter audio in) simultaneously.

## Sample & Hold

The S&H captures and holds the input voltage at the moment of a trigger rising edge. The output steps through random voltages when driven by noise (classic random CV). With a clock at TRIG IN, it samples rhythmically. With the internal clock (no external trigger), the RATE knob controls sample frequency.

## LFO

Five simultaneous waveform outputs share a single RATE knob. The LFO runs continuously (no sync input). Each output can be used independently as a modulation source.

| Waveform | Character |
| --- | --- |
| Sine | Smooth, rounded modulation |
| Triangle | Linear rise and fall — similar to sine but with sharper peaks |
| Sawtooth | Falls slowly, snaps up — good for filter sweeps |
| Ramp | Rises slowly, snaps down — good for upward sweeps |
| Square | Hard switching between two values — gate-like |
