# Overview

**WEST PEST** is a fully analog west coast-style semi-modular synthesizer developed by Cre8audio in partnership with Pittsburgh Modular Synthesizers. It packs eight discrete modules into one 40 HP panel, internally patched into a complete voice that can be rerouted via 18 Eurorack-compatible patch points.

The core oscillator technology descends from Pittsburgh Modular's Voltage Research Laboratory, evolved through their SV-1 and optimized here for wavefolding. Rather than clipping harmonics, the wavefolder reflects the waveform back onto itself — up to six folds — adding dense harmonic complexity without the grit of conventional distortion.

Articulation is handled by the Dynamics Controller: a circuit inspired by Don Buchla's work and refined by Pittsburgh's founders that combines filter, VCA, and envelope in a single unit. Triggering it produces a sharp, open attack followed by a ringing, resonant decay — simultaneously bowed and percussive. The character shifts from a low-pass gate to a more envelope-forward feel depending on the RESONANCE setting.

Additional tools include a digital multi-mod section (switchable LFO, envelope, random, and MIDI-synced modes), a MIDI-to-CV converter with 5-pin DIN input, an 8-step sequencer with arpeggiator, and a 13-button keyboard. The entire instrument can be driven by MIDI, CV/gate, the internal sequencer, or the button keys.
