# Tips

- **Start with FOLD at noon.** At minimum it's essentially bypassed; at maximum the signal can become very dense or even quieter as folds cancel out. The sweet spot for most sounds is around 9 o'clock to 2 o'clock.

- **The Dynamics Controller is not a standard VCF.** It behaves more like a low-pass gate — it responds to amplitude as well as frequency. High RESONANCE adds a ringing metallic quality to every note. Keep RESONANCE below noon for more conventional sounds.

- **Use STRIKE for percussive layering.** The manual STRIKE button triggers the Dynamics Controller at any time, useful for adding accents or finger-drumming while the sequencer runs.

- **Multi-mod in Random mode + quantiser = generative melody.** Route multi-mod OUT into a quantiser input and then to an external VCO for a clocked random melody without a sequencer.

- **Break the normal for hybrid patches.** Patch a cable into AUDIO IN on the Dynamics Controller to disconnect the wavefolder and run an external signal through the LPG-style circuit.

- **Clock sync the multi-mod LFO for rhythmic modulation.** In LFO Sync mode, the multi-mod rate locks to divisions/multiples of the sequencer tempo, keeping modulation musically in time.

- **External audio into AUDIO IN (oscillator section).** The oscillator section's AUDIO IN feeds into the wavefolder, so you can fold external audio — drums, other VCOs, anything — through the West Pest's circuit.

- **Power draw is significant.** At 304 mA +12 V and 182 mA −12 V, budget carefully in smaller cases.
