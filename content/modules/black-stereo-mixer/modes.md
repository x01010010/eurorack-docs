# Modes

**BLACK STEREO MIXER V2** has no modal switching beyond the per-channel options below.

## Standard Stereo Mix

By default all four inputs contribute to the L and R output buses according to their level controls. IN1–IN3 preserve the stereo field of their inputs; IN4 is placed in the stereo field by the PAN knob.

## +6 dB Boost (IN1, IN2)

Engaging the BOOST switch on IN1 or IN2 adds 6 dB of gain on that channel. Use this when feeding the mixer from a module with a lower-than-normal output level — small-format oscillators, battery-powered synths via EXT inputs, or effects modules that run at instrument level.

## L/R Channel Merge (IN1, IN2)

The MERGE switch sums the left and right inputs of IN1 and/or IN2 together and sends the result to both sides of the bus. This is useful when a stereo input is actually carrying two independent mono signals (e.g., the two outputs of a dual oscillator) and you want both voices to appear center-panned in the mix rather than hard-panned.

## CV Panning (IN4)

IN4 accepts a mono signal and distributes it between L and R according to the PAN knob position, modulated by the PAN CV jack. Slow LFO modulation creates auto-pan effects; fast modulation or random CV creates tremolo-like amplitude variation in the stereo field.
