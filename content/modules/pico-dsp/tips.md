# Tips

## Set PARAM 1 to Center for Maximum CV Range

The CV input is summed with the PARAM 1 knob. With the knob at center (noon), a ±5 V CV sweeps the full parameter range in both directions. With the knob at an extreme, the CV can only push in one direction.

## Use the Colour Coding in the Dark

The colour-coded effect selector is readable by feel once memorised — useful for live performance in low light. Note the positions of your two or three most-used effects before a set.

## Stereo Spread with a Single Module

Even from a mono patch, **PICO DSP** outputs true stereo. In stereo delay or Leslie mode, left and right carry genuinely different signals. Routing both outputs to a stereo mixer channel or stereo recorder immediately widens a mono patch.

## Granular Delay as a Pitch Scrambler

At extreme PARAM 1 settings on Granular Delay, the grain size becomes large enough to introduce pitch artifacts and stutters. Use this at the edge of feedback for unpredictable self-generating textures.

## Parallel Wet with a Mixer

For subtle effects that complement rather than replace the dry signal, use a mixer to blend **PICO DSP** wet output with the original dry signal. Set DRY/WET to fully wet for cleaner separation. This also lets you apply different processing to different VCOs — one dry, one through the DSP.

## Saturated Reverb for Glued Drums

Run a drum mix into Saturated Reverb with a short decay. The saturation glues the reverb tail to the transient and prevents the reverb from becoming too clean or distracting. Dial DRY/WET back to around 30–40% for a natural room sound with character.
