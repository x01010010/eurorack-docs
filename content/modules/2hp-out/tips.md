# Tips & Tricks

## Set Line Level Once, Adjust Headphone Separately

The most common workflow error with output modules is adjusting the line level when you only intend to change headphone volume. Dial in your LINE VOL for a consistent recording level, then treat HP VOL as your personal monitoring control that you can change freely without affecting what goes to tape.

## Use the LEDs as a Gain Staging Check

The L and R input LEDs reflect the modular-level signal before the volume controls. If they are very dim, your mix is arriving too quiet and downstream noise will be more audible. If they are constantly bright and clipping, pull back the levels feeding **OUT** rather than turning down the output volume — distortion happens before the volume control.

## 3.5 mm to Other Formats

The line output uses a 3.5 mm (TRS) jack. Use a 3.5 mm to 6.35 mm (¼") adapter or cable to connect to standard studio gear. The signal is stereo on a TRS connector — verify the cable/adapter supports stereo rather than splitting to mono.

## Headphone Impedance

**OUT** drives standard consumer and semi-pro headphones (32–250 Ω) well. Very high impedance headphones (600 Ω) may sound quieter than expected. If headphone volume is insufficient, consider a dedicated headphone amplifier downstream.
