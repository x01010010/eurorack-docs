# Modes

The 112 has no operating modes in the digital sense — both VCOs run continuously. The "modes" here are the practical configurations the panel encourages.

## Two independent voices

The most basic configuration. Patch each V/OCT separately, take each VCO's outputs to its own filter chain. You have a true two-voice analog synth in 16 HP.

## Detuned unison

Patch the same V/OCT into both VCO 1 and VCO 2 KBD CV IN. Slightly offset their TUNE knobs. Mix the two SAW outputs at the same level for chorused supersaw, or run TRI + SAW for a rounder tone.

## Hard sync (strong)

VCO 1 SYNC OUT → VCO 2 SYNC IN, sync switch on **STRONG**. VCO 2's pitch knob now sweeps formant character against VCO 1's fundamental. Modulate VCO 2 PITCH with an envelope for the classic sync-sweep lead.

## Soft sync (weak)

Same patching, switch on **WEAK**. VCO 2 stays partially independent — you keep more of its harmonics while it loosely locks to VCO 1. Useful for thicker, less aggressive sync sounds.

## Audio-rate cross-mod / FM

Patch VCO 1 TRI OUT into VCO 2 MOD CV IN 2, advance MOD MANUAL. As VCO 1's frequency rises into audio rate, VCO 2's pitch is modulated at audio frequencies — analog FM. Triangle is the cleanest FM source; saw and pulse give bell-like sidebands.

## PWM duets

Patch a slow LFO (or **WAVES** unipolar out) into PWM IN on both VCOs. Set their PW knobs to differ slightly. The two pulse outputs phase against each other for a wide, breathing pad sound.
