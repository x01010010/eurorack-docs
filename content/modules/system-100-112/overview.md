# Overview

**112** is Behringer's Eurorack reproduction of the **Roland System 100M Model 112 Dual VCO** from the early 1980s. Two independent analog oscillator cores share a single 16 HP panel, each with three waveform outputs, PWM, and a flexible CV summing input section.

It is one of the densest dual VCOs in Behringer's System 100 line and a workhorse sound source for analog leads, pads, basses, and FM-style timbres.

## What you get

- **Two independent VCO cores** with shared panel layout
- **Three waveforms per core** — triangle (bipolar), sawtooth (unipolar), pulse with PWM (unipolar)
- **Octave selector** per VCO covering 32′, 16′, 8′, 4′, 2′
- **Three CV inputs per VCO** at 1 V/oct, summed at the input stage
- **PWM CV input** per VCO with a manual width control
- **Weak and strong sync** between the cores for hard sync and softer phase locking
- **Dedicated audio outputs** — two per VCO

## Authentic 100M, with caveats

The circuit is faithful to the original Roland design — including the broad **2-octave tune knob** range, which makes precise tuning fussy, and the use of an LM3046 transistor pair without active temperature compensation. Practically, this means **the 112 will drift with case temperature**. Plan to retune after a warm-up period, especially in temperature-variable rooms.

For studio work where tracking matters across long sessions, consider a small fan in the case or a brief warm-up before recording.

## What it sounds like

Classic Roland 100M character — fat, slightly woody triangle, biting sawtooth, and a hollow pulse that comes alive under PWM. Two cores in a 16 HP module gives you instant detune layering for thick supersaw or unison-style sounds.
