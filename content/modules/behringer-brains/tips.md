# Tips & Tricks

- **OUT2 is not a copy** — it carries a different signal for almost every engine. Always patch it somewhere, even temporarily, to discover what each engine provides there; many are worth routing independently.
- **Use the MODEL CV for timbral sequencing** — a slow stepped CV through the MODEL input cycles engines in sync with your sequence, effectively "changing instruments" mid-pattern without touching the panel.
- **TRIG resets phase in analogue models** — for tight ensemble playing with other oscillators, patch the same gate to TRIG on all voices to lock phase on each note.
- **Physical models decay on their own** — for Bank C engines the TRIG input excites the resonator and the sound decays naturally. Do not gate the output; let the resonator ring. VCA gating kills the natural tail.
- **Calibrate with a known V/oct source** — long-press BANK (hold BANK, then press MODEL) to enter pitch calibration. Send 1V and 3V from a known-accurate source to calibrate the tracking across the full range.
- **FM attenuverter at noon = no FM** — the FM attenuverter is a true attenuverter (bipolar); at noon it passes nothing. Turn CW for positive FM depth, CCW for inverted FM.
