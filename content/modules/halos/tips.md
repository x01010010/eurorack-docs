# Tips & Tricks

- **STRUM without audio in = internal exciter** — Halos has a built-in noise/impulse exciter. Leaving IN unpatched and using only STRUM is valid and produces cleaner, more controlled tones than external excitation.
- **POSITION is the secret timbre knob** — moving POSITION from 0 to 1 changes which partials are excited (like moving a bow or pick position on a string). Noon gives a balanced tone; extremes emphasise or suppress certain harmonics dramatically.
- **High BRIGHTNESS + high DAMPING = fast metallic ping** — set BRIGHTNESS near maximum and DAMPING near minimum for a short, bell-like ping on every strum. Great as a melodic percussion voice.
- **Low STRUCTURE = stable pitch; high STRUCTURE = inharmonic** — STRUCTURE below 9 o'clock keeps partials in harmonic relationships. Above that, partials spread into inharmonic territory — metallic, gong-like textures. For melodic use, keep STRUCTURE below noon.
- **4-voice mode has shorter resonance** — Halos divides its computational budget across voices. With 4 voices active, each has a shorter maximum decay than in mono mode. For long, sustained tones use mono or 2-voice.
- **ODD/EVEN as a built-in EQ split** — before patching to a mixer, send ODD and EVEN to separate channels and apply different EQ per channel. You get a free spectral split that lets you control the brightness mix without an additional filter.
