# Overview

**A-124-2** is the slim-line 4 HP version of Doepfer's A-124 Wasp filter. Both modules implement the same circuit: a 12 dB/oct multimode filter derived from the filter in the EDP Wasp synthesizer (1978, Electronic Dream Plant, UK). The Wasp's original designer Chris Huggett used standard digital inverter chips (CD4069) wired as analog op-amps — an unconventional and technically incorrect use that produces a distinctive character: slightly gritty, warm, and harmonically dense in a way that conventional op-amp filter designs do not.

The filter provides two outputs: a dedicated Bandpass out and a Mix output that blends LP and HP signals. The Mix knob sweeps from pure LP through Notch (equal LP+HP cancellation) to pure HP. The A-124-2 adds a third output socket which carries either LP or HP alone, selected by an internal jumper.

Unlike most Doepfer filters, **A-124-2** does not self-oscillate at maximum resonance. The resonance adds thickness and emphasis but the filter remains stable. This makes it forgiving to drive hard — the characteristic grit comes from the circuit topology rather than resonance feedback.

The A-124-2 differs from the A-124/A-124 SE only in its panel size (4 HP vs 8 HP) and the use of small trimmer-style controls for Mix and Input Level in place of full-size knobs.

## At a Glance

- **4 HP**, 45 mm deep (Slim Line Series)
- **12 dB/oct multimode**: LP, BP, HP, Notch (via mix)
- **Wasp circuit** — CD4069 digital inverters as op-amps
- **No self-oscillation**
- **3 outputs**: Bandpass, LP/HP Mix, LP or HP (jumper)
- **2× CV inputs** with attenuators
- **Power**: 40 mA (+12 V) / 10 mA (−12 V)
