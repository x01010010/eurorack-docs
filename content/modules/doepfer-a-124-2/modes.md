# Modes

**A-124-2** has no mode switch — the available filter responses are determined by which output jack is used and the position of the MIX knob.

## Low-Pass Mode

Use MIX OUT with the MIX knob fully CCW. The output carries a 12 dB/oct low-pass response. Alternatively set the LP/HP OUT jumper to LP and use that jack directly.

## High-Pass Mode

Use MIX OUT with the MIX knob fully CW. The output carries a 12 dB/oct high-pass response. Or set the LP/HP OUT jumper to HP.

## Notch (Band-Reject) Mode

Set MIX OUT with the MIX knob at center (noon). Equal LP and HP signals cancel the mid-frequency content and produce a notch. The notch frequency tracks with FREQ. Sweeping FREQ moves the null point through the spectrum.

## Bandpass Mode

Use BP OUT. The 12 dB bandpass is always available at this jack regardless of the MIX knob position. Resonance adds emphasis around the center frequency.

## LP/HP Mix (Morphing)

The MIX knob continuously morphs the MIX OUT between LP and HP responses via notch at the midpoint. Automating this with CV (patched to CV2 and dialed in with its attenuator) creates a continuously shifting filter character — sweeping from dark LP through hollow notch to bright HP.

## Resonance Character

At low resonance the Wasp sound is subtle — a slight mid-peak and warmth that differs from clean ladder or SVF designs. As resonance increases the circuit begins to distort in a musical way. Maximum resonance produces a pronounced peak but does not self-oscillate; the CD4069 circuit clips instead, adding harmonic saturation.
