# Tips & Tricks

- **Tap tempo is the most underused feature** — for delay algorithms, tap the TAP button 4 times in rhythm before your sequence starts. Delays will be rhythmically in time without any calculation or external clock patching.
- **Mono in, stereo out = free stereo widening** — even the reverb and modulation algorithms expand mono signals to wide stereo. Route any mono source through Space FX just for stereo width, even at minimal wet levels.
- **Short reverb times for glue** — room and hall algorithms at very short reverb times (0.2–0.5 s) add subtle ambience and cohesion to a mix without making individual elements swim in reverb. Useful when you want "presence" not "space."
- **High feedback delay for infinite repeats** — push delay FEEDBACK (PARAM 2) close to maximum for near-infinite echoes. Use sparingly — it builds into a wash quickly. Reduce feedback during a transition to clear the space.
- **Combination reverb+delay saves HP** — use combination algorithm programs when you need both reverb and delay in limited rack space. Less flexible than separate modules, but saves 20+ HP in a small case.
- **LEVEL switch for parallel processing** — leave LEVEL at 50% wet. Route the dry signal from your source separately to the mixer alongside OUT L/R. You have both the dry signal and the wet effect in parallel, giving full control over the wet/dry ratio at the mixer.
