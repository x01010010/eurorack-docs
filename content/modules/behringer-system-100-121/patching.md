# Patch Recipes

## Classic Subtractive Voice

Patch a VCO to SIG 1 on filter 1. Patch an envelope to MOD 1 (with MOD 1 LEVEL at about 2 o'clock). Set LP mode, moderate resonance. Patch another envelope to a VCA after the filter output. This is the foundational subtractive synth signal chain.

## Stereo Filter Pair

Patch the left channel of a stereo source to filter 1 SIG 1 and the right to filter 2 SIG 1. Set both filters identically for matched stereo filtering, or deliberately detune CUTOFF slightly between the two for subtle stereo width and movement.

## Bandpass from LP + HP in Series

Set filter 1 to HP with cutoff at the lower boundary of the desired band. Set filter 2 to LP with cutoff at the upper boundary. Route filter 1 OUT to filter 2 SIG 1. The result is a steep bandpass filter with the band defined by the two cutoff points.

## Keyboard Tracking for Melodic VCF

Patch the sequencer V/OCT to the V/OCT input on the filter. The cutoff tracks the played pitch, keeping the filter tone consistent across the keyboard range. Particularly effective for lead voices where you want the filter brightness proportional to pitch.

## Dual Source Mixing into Single Filter

Patch a VCO to SIG 1 and a noise source to SIG 2. Adjust the respective LEVEL knobs to mix them before filtering. One filter handles both signal types simultaneously with individual level control — no need for a separate mixer.

## Self-Oscillation Melody

Turn RESONANCE fully clockwise on both filters, set both to LP. Patch V/OCT to both V/OCT inputs and a gate to an envelope → MOD 1. The two filters sing as sine oscillators. Detune their CUTOFF slightly for a chorus effect.
