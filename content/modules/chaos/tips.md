# Tips & Tricks

- **LENGTH is the most musical control** — short LENGTH gives a repeating phrase (familiar, structured); long LENGTH gives chaos (organic but disorganised). The sweet spot is medium LENGTH — long enough to feel generative, short enough to feel purposeful. Start there and adjust.
- **STEPS CCW for smooth modulation, CW for melody** — fully smooth X outputs work best for modulation (filter, pan, FM); stepped or quantised outputs work best for pitch CV. Most useful patches use STEPS somewhere in between.
- **T section as a probability gate** — at low JITTER and medium BIAS, T1/T2/T3 behave like probability-weighted gate sources. Program a kick on T1 (favoured), snare on T2 (medium), and accent on T3 (rare) for a generative drum pattern that feels composed.
- **Y for macro evolution** — unlike X outputs (which can be fast-stepping), Y changes slowly and smoothly. Patch Y to reverb time, delay feedback, or LFO rate for slow, organic macro-level changes that feel like the patch is "breathing."
- **Lock + unlock loop during performance** — turn LENGTH to minimum to lock a "favourite" random phrase. Play with it for 8 bars. Then slowly increase LENGTH to let the phrase evolve. This live morphing technique works well for builds.
- **FEEDBACK for pitch coherence** — with low FEEDBACK, X1/X2/X3 are independent and can jump to unrelated pitches. High FEEDBACK makes all three cluster together in pitch — useful when you want all voices to stay in a similar register rather than jumping octaves.
