# Tips & Tricks

- **Use both FM inputs for layered modulation** — FM1 and FM2 sum at the cutoff. Patch a fast envelope to FM1 for note attack and a slow LFO to FM2 for ongoing movement. The filter responds to both simultaneously.
- **INPUT LEVEL as a drive knob** — increasing INPUT LEVEL past noon drives the filter harder, adding harmonic saturation before the resonant circuit. Use this for analogue warmth and subtle clipping on bass and lead voices.
- **4-pole LP for bass; 2-pole LP for mid/high voices** — the 4-pole setting removes high-frequency content aggressively, which sounds great on bass but can dull high-frequency voices. Switch to 2-pole for voices that need presence and brightness above the cutoff.
- **BP for formant-like filtering** — the band-pass output with high resonance produces a narrow, resonant peak that sounds like a vocal formant. Sweep it with an LFO or envelope for wah-type effects; patch two Surges for a dual-formant vowel-like filter.
- **Self-oscillation + V/OCT = free sine oscillator** — the self-oscillating filter tracks V/OCT and produces a clean sine wave. Use it as a sine VCO when you need one, without dedicating a separate oscillator module.
- **Partial keyboard tracking** — attenuate the V/OCT signal before patching (e.g., through an attenuverter at 50%). The filter cutoff rises at half the rate of pitch. Higher notes get slightly brighter but not fully tracking — a natural-sounding partial cutoff tracking for pads and strings.
