# Tips & Tricks

- **Use outputs 1 and 5 for perfect inverses** — in Single mode, outputs 1 and 5 are always 180° apart regardless of waveform. This gives you a free inverted copy of any LFO shape without an attenuverter.
- **Outputs 1 and 3 for quadrature** — 0° and 90° is the classic quadrature pairing. Use them together on X/Y CV inputs for circular or Lissajous-style modulation paths (filter cutoff + resonance, or stereo panning + depth).
- **WAVE CV from a sequencer in Multi mode** — clocking a sequencer into WAVE CV steps through waveform rotations at a musical rhythm, producing structured shifts in which modulation shapes reach which destinations. More predictable than slow LFO feedback.
- **FM IN for rate expression** — patch an envelope into FM IN with FM LEVEL at a low setting. Short events briefly speed up the LFO, adding a subtle rush of modulation rate on transients — analogous to tape flutter or physical vibration response.
- **Unipolar mode for VCA and filter** — many VCAs and filters expect 0–+5 V control voltage. Switch to unipolar mode (RATE to 12 o'clock, flip switch 6×) when driving these destinations to avoid negative voltages closing the VCA unexpectedly.
- **Freeze for snapshot composition** — set RATE to noon and manually advance the wave by bumping it CCW or CW from noon to scan to a new frozen position. Works well for slowly evolving drone patches where you want infrequent, deliberate voltage changes.
- **Clock sync for LFO-as-rhythmic-tool** — synced to a slow clock (e.g. one pulse per bar), the Octasource's eight outputs provide eight rhythmically-related modulation shapes. Assign them to filter, pitch, delay, reverb, and VCA for a patch that breathes with the tempo.
- **Stack both modes** — use two Octasources: one in Single mode for phased sweeps, one in Multi mode for waveform variety. Cross-patch outputs from the Multi-mode unit into CV inputs on the Single-mode unit for a dense, generative modulation matrix.
