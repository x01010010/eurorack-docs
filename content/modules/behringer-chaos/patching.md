# Patch Recipes

## Generative Melody

Patch X1 to a VCO V/OCT, T1 to a VCA envelope trigger. Set STEPS near CW for quantised voltages and SPREAD to about noon. Set LENGTH to a medium value for a repeating-but-varied melodic cell. Adjust BIAS to control how often notes fire.

## Polyrhythmic Drum Pattern

Patch T1, T2, T3 to three different drum voices (kick, snare, hi-hat or similar). Set RATE to musical tempo. Adjust BIAS to favour the kick on T1 or hi-hat on T3. JITTER adds subtle humanisation. A complete three-voice drum pattern from one module.

## Correlated Pitch + Filter CV

Patch X1 to a VCO pitch and X2 to a VCF cutoff. Set FEEDBACK high — X1 and X2 will produce related but different voltages. Bright filter notes tend to follow high pitches; dark filter notes tend to follow low pitches. A natural-feeling correlation.

## Locked Loop Motif

Turn LENGTH fully CCW (minimum). Chaos locks to a short repeating loop — a fixed random pattern. Use this as a generative sequencer with a fixed phrase length. Advance LENGTH slowly over time to "evolve" the pattern during a performance.

## Slow Random Modulation

Patch Y output to a reverb mix or chorus depth. Y is a smooth, slow-moving CV derived from the T clock. At low RATE it changes slowly — useful for slow, organic modulation that doesn't sound mechanical. Independent from the stepped X outputs.

## External Clock with Random Fills

Patch a master clock to EXT CLOCK. Set JITTER near noon. The sequence locks to your master tempo but individual hits drift slightly — a humanised, "live drummer" feel in the timing without going fully random.
