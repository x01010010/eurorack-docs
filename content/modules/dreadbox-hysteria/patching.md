# Patch Recipes

## Classic Sync Lead

Patch a second oscillator (tuned lower) into SYNC. Use Hysteria's 1V/OCT for the melody. Modulate the CV input with an envelope or LFO to sweep the harmonic content while the fundamental stays locked to the sync source.

## Self-Clocking Sequencer

Take the PULSE output into your sequencer's clock input. Hysteria's frequency becomes the tempo — turn the OCT slider down for slow clocks, up for fast. Useful for creating tempo-locked patches without a dedicated clock module.

## Waveshape Animation

Patch an LFO into the WAVEFORM CV input. Set the LFO to a slow triangle or random. The timbre continuously evolves as the waveshaper sweeps through its range — especially effective on pads and drones.

## Quantized Random Melody

Patch a sample-and-hold or random voltage source into the 1V/OCT input with the quantizer engaged. Every random voltage snaps to a semitone, generating atonal-but-musical melodies. Use the OCT slider to constrain the range.

## Detuned Pair

Use two Hysterias (or Hysteria + another VCO). Tune one slightly sharp or flat using the SEMI slider in unquantized mode. Pan them left and right for thick stereo detuning, or mix them to a single output for classic analog fatness.

## FM Drone

Patch an audio-rate source into the CV input with the attenuator at a low setting. Leave the quantizer off. Adjust the CV knob to taste — small amounts add subtle harmonics, larger amounts produce clangorous FM tones.
