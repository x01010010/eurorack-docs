# Tips & Tricks

- **Try all six routings before settling** — the same knob settings sound genuinely different across each routing. When designing a patch, cycle through all three with ROUTING before tweaking parameters; the right routing is often more impactful than any individual knob adjustment.
- **VOLUME/DRIVE as a character control** — past roughly 3 o'clock the output stage adds its own saturation. Use this as a final "glue" or warmth control even when the DISTORTION block is off or minimal.
- **Freeze as a performance tool** — the FREEZE jack lets you hold the reverb buffer via CV. Gate it from a sequencer or envelope to "snapshot" a moment of audio and sustain it while your patch evolves around it.
- **ENV OUT for free modulation** — the sidechain envelope output is a useful free LFO/AR envelope that fires on any trigger patched to SIDECHAIN TRIG. Even if you're not ducking anything, patch TRIG from a clock and take ENV OUT to modulate another module.
- **TONE/GAIN for input matching** — if your source is quiet, hold ROUTING and turn TONE/GAIN clockwise past 12 o'clock for a clean input gain boost before everything else. Saves having to use a separate utility module.
- **Tilt EQ at 12 o'clock is truly flat** — unlike many tone controls that add colour at centre, the tilt EQ is completely neutral at noon. Use it confidently for quick spectral shaping without affecting overall level.
- **Time CV for expressive pitch-shifting** — at short delay times with high feedback, sweeping the TIME CV produces pitch-shifting effects. Combined with 1V/oct tracking from a sequencer, GHOST acts as a rudimentary pitch-based synthesiser voice.
- **Compressor after routing** — the compressor always sits after the three main blocks regardless of the ROUTING selection. Set it last after you've established your routing and gain staging so the preset values work as intended.
