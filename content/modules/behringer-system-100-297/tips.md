# Tips

- **Asymmetric glide is the killer feature.** Set RISE slow and FALL fast for a natural pitch-drop effect; reverse for a note-attack slide that resolves quickly.

- **Stack portamento and CV mixer.** Mix two pitch sources in CV Mixer 1, then feed the result into Port A for a merged, gliding pitch voice.

- **Use at audio rate cautiously.** The slew circuits are not designed for audio-rate processing, but slow square waves can be shaped into rough triangles at the edge of audio range.

- **CV Mixer attenuators as scalers.** Even when mixing a single source, the attenuator knobs on the CV mixers let you reduce modulation depth without needing a dedicated attenuator module.

- **Pair with the 140 envelope.** Feed the 140's envelope output through a portamento channel for a softened attack/decay shape — useful for reducing click on percussive patches.

- **RISE at zero = immediate attack.** With FALL set and RISE fully counterclockwise, the portamento channel passes rising edges through unchanged but slews falling edges, effectively acting as a decay-only lag.
