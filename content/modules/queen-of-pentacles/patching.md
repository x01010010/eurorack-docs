# Patch Recipes

## Sequenced 909-Style Kit

Patch trigger outputs from a sequencer or clock divider into the TRIG IN jacks — BD on the downbeat, SD on beats 2 and 4, closed hi-hat on every 8th note. Set BD DRIVE and DECAY for a punchy, modern kick; SD TUNE for snare brightness. Dial CABIN PRESSURE into a plate reverb on the snare only (enable its effect switch) for depth.

## Live Drum Roll Performance

Patch an external clock (e.g. from Pico Trigger) into CLOCK IN. During a live set, hold any channel's TRIG button to fire a roll at the incoming clock rate. Combine a BD roll and an S1 cymbal roll simultaneously for a fill. Release both to drop back into the regular sequence.

## Accent Velocity Dynamics

Patch a sequencer's accent CV output into ACCENT IN on the BD channel. Program high accent on the first beat of every bar and lower velocity elsewhere. The bass drum hits harder on the accented steps, bringing dynamics and groove into an otherwise static pattern.

## Effect Processor as Send/Return

Patch the master stereo output from another module (or a separate drum machine) into the AUX IN jack. Route it through the effect processor with CABIN PRESSURE fully wet. The Queen of Pentacles' internal voices pass through the effect alongside the external signal.

## Sample Bank Live Switching

Load each of the 8 banks with different stylistic drum kits (electronic, acoustic, lo-fi, etc.). Long-press the MODE button on S1 to cycle banks during performance. The kit transitions immediately — useful for dramatic section changes.

## Isolator Filter Performance

Automate the master FILTER knob (or patch a slow LFO into FILTER CV) for classic DJ-style isolator sweeps. Starting from 12 o'clock (flat), sweep CCW to roll off highs for a bass-heavy buildup, then back to flat for the drop. Sweep CW for a thin, filtered breakdown.

## Individual Outs to External FX

Patch BD OUT, SD OUT, and CP OUT to separate channels of an external mixer or audio interface. Each voice can receive its own EQ, compression, and reverb independently — the Queen of Pentacles becomes a multichannel audio interface for your drum programming.
