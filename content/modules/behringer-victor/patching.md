# Patch Recipes

## Envelope-Swept Vector Pad

Load four complementary waveforms at the oscillator corners (e.g., sine, triangle, sawtooth, pulse). Patch an ADSR envelope to X IN. As the note plays, the vector sweeps from one oscillator to another, changing timbre over the note's duration — from attack to release, the character evolves automatically.

## LFO Circle Modulation

Patch a 0°/90° quadrature LFO pair (e.g., from Four LFO in Quadrature mode) to X IN and Y IN. The vector position traces a circle through the four oscillators, creating continuous timbre rotation — useful for evolving pads and drones.

## Joystick Expressive Performance

Leave X IN and Y IN unpatched. Play with the joystick in real time while holding notes. Move between the four oscillator corners to change the timbre expressively mid-note. Record the joystick movements via CV recording if your sequencer supports it.

## MIX + X/Y as Three Voices

Patch MIX, X, and Y to three separate VCFs or effects chains. MIX gets the blended vector signal; X and Y carry the intermediate X/Y blend signals. Three distinct but harmonically related timbres from one oscillator module.

## Waveform Morphing Bass

Select four similar waveforms with subtle differences (e.g., four slightly different pulse widths or sine/triangle variants). Keep the joystick near the centre. At slow CV sweep rates through X/Y, the timbre morphs continuously — an organic, slowly evolving bass texture.

## FM into Vector

Patch an FM signal to the FM input while vector-sweeping. The FM adds harmonic complexity on top of the waveform blend — combine an evolving vector sweep with FM modulation depth CV for very dense, animated timbres.
