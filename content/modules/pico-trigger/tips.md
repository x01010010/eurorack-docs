# Tips & Tricks

- **Upload from your phone** — the web-based pattern designer works on mobile browsers. Connect your phone's headphone output to the CLK jack with a 3.5 mm cable for on-the-go pattern changes without a laptop.
- **Volume matters for upload** — set your device's audio output to maximum volume before uploading patterns. Low volume can cause failed transfers. The encoder LED will not blink green if the upload didn't take.
- **Layer multiple Pico Triggers** — since the module is only 3 HP, two or three of them give you 8–12 trigger tracks with independent pattern lengths and shuffle settings. Sync them by patching CLK OUT from the master into CLK on the others.
- **Use short patterns for generative loops** — a track set to 3 steps against another at 5 steps gives a 15-step super-pattern before repeating. Four tracks at prime-number lengths (3, 5, 7, 11) produce a 1,155-step cycle.
- **Shuffle as a sound design tool** — heavy shuffle values on fast tempos create flam-like double hits that add texture to drum sounds, especially on hi-hats and metallic percussion.
- **Pattern switching for song structure** — prepare 8 patterns in advance (intro, verse, chorus, fill, etc.) and use the LOAD button to switch between them live. Switching is immediate and quantized to the next step.
- **Backup your patterns** — the web designer doesn't save state between sessions. Screenshot or note down your patterns before closing the browser, since there's no way to read patterns back from the module.
