# Overview

**ELMYRA 2** is a 4-voice digital/analog hybrid drone synthesizer from Neutral Labs, designed as a platform for microtonal sonic exploration. It ships as a 42 HP Eurorack module or in a standalone bamboo desktop case with USB-C power. At its heart is a wavetable-based complex oscillator engine with up to 12 oscillators in total, feeding a switchable filter, delay, reverb, and the analog OUCH distortion circuit.

Each of the four voices can be triggered by touch pads or CV, has its own polymetric sequencer, and supports ten per-voice effects including sub-oscillators, waveshaping, saturation, bit-mangling, and sample rate reduction. With 41 patch points and 31 modulation targets the **ELMYRA 2** is closer to a small semi-modular system than a single module — everything feeds back into everything else.

It works equally well for long-form drone and ambient compositions, gritty industrial textures, microtonal melodic structures, and pure noise experiments. Preset cards (a set of four included) or bare components such as diodes or capacitors can be plugged into the front panel jumper inserts to reshape the sound character without any patching.

The **ELMYRA 2** is the successor to the original Elmyra, expanding the architecture with more voices, a reworked oscillator engine, the ladder filter option, polyphonic chord mode, more modulation routing, and deeper sequencing.

## At a Glance

- **42 HP**, 4-voice wavetable/analog hybrid
- **4 voices** — touch or CV activated, 1 V/oct compatible
- **Up to 12 oscillators** total across all voices
- **31 modulation targets**, **41 patch points**
- **Switchable filter** — 2-pole SVF (LP/BP/HP) or 4-pole ladder LP
- **Delay, lo-fi reverb, OUCH** analog distortion circuit
- **Polymetric sequencers** — up to 128 steps per voice
- **Microtonal scales** — 7, 9, 15, 17, and 12-tone chromatic
- **2 complex LFOs** — free, sync'd, sample & hold, sample & glide
- **External audio input**
- Power: `<100 mA` +12 V, `0 mA` −12 V (or USB-C 5 V for desktop)
