# Tips

**Start without patching.** Touch the pads, let the internal sequencers run, explore filter and OUCH settings. **ELMYRA 2** is fully functional with zero patch cables — understand the internal architecture before adding CV complexity.

**Use different step counts for polymetric drift.** Set voice 1 to 3 steps, voice 2 to 5, voice 3 to 7, voice 4 to 11. The same clock drives all four, but their patterns phase against each other over a long cycle — effectively a slowly evolving generative texture with no randomness required.

**Sub-oscillators for effortless bass support.** Enable dual sub-oscillators on one or two voices and tune them lower than the others. The subs fill the low register without requiring a separate bass voice or module.

**The OUCH circuit responds dramatically to preset cards.** Swap the included cards while a drone is running and listen to how the character shifts. Each card changes the distortion curve and filtering character in a way that isn't easily replicated by knob adjustments alone.

**Ladder filter with high resonance = self-oscillation.** Push the ladder LP's resonance while reducing cutoff frequency — the filter begins to ring at its own pitch. This pitch is CV-controllable, turning the filter itself into an additional tone. Pair with the OUCH circuit for a particularly gnarly character.

**Drive the delay into self-oscillation.** Push delay feedback to maximum while modulating delay time with an LFO. The delay becomes a pitch source. Drop the feedback back down periodically to prevent the loop from overwhelming everything.

**Envelope outputs are powerful modulation sources.** The four touch-pad envelopes are not just for gating voices — patch them to filter cutoff, delay feedback, LFO depth, or other voices' levels. Each touch-pad interaction ripples through the entire signal path.

**LFO S&H mode + clock sync = stepped random.** Lock an LFO to the sequencer clock in Sample & Hold mode. Each clock tick samples a new voltage from the LFO cycle — effectively a random CV source locked to your tempo. Patch this to pitch for rhythmically locked pitch randomization.

**External audio into the filter is a legitimate effect unit.** Feed drum machines, other synths, or samples into the Audio In. The full effects chain — filter, OUCH, delay, reverb — applies to external signals. **ELMYRA 2** can serve as a dedicated effects module for the rest of a rack.

**Scale mode unlocks harmonic structures.** Switch to chromatic mode and choose a 7 or 9-tone microtonal scale. With voices stepping through the same scale at different rates, you get harmonically coherent but rhythmically complex polymelodic textures without needing a quantizer.

**The CV summing utility is undersold.** Combine a slow LFO with a fast touch-envelope, sum them, and send to a voice's pitch input. The result is a drift that breathes at LFO rate but spikes with each touch — a much more alive modulation than either source alone.

**Firmware updates add significant features.** v2.4 introduced higher sample rate wavetable resolution, new wavetables, an overhauled delay and reverb, expanded filter modes, and oscillator hard sync. Check neutral-labs.com regularly for new firmware.
