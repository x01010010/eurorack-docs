# Tips

**Start with the blend control at 0% (fully dry).** This gives you a dry reference. Slowly raise the blend to hear how much distortion you're adding. Many users discover they only need 10–30% blend for the effect to be audible and musical.

**Use INPUT LEVEL to control how hard you hit the distortion.** Higher input = more saturation. Lower input = cleaner, more controlled distortion. This is often more musical than cranking the gain knobs all the way.

**Diode fuzz = warm and classic. MOSFET fuzz = aggressive and modern.** Flip the switch while playing to hear the difference. Diode is forgiving; MOSFET demands respect.

**The FET preamp alone is often enough.** Many users apply only FET Gain (Fuzz Gain down, BLEND ~15–30%) for a transparent fattening effect. Save the fuzz for moments where you want attitude.

**Baxandall EQ is musical at all settings.** Unlike parametric EQ, it won't create dips or harsh peaks. Boost liberally. Shelving EQs are designed to be transparent even when heavily tweaked.

**Use HIGH EQ boost to brighten fuzz.** Fuzz naturally reduces high-end definition. Boost the HIGH EQ while using fuzz to restore presence and clarity.

**Use LOW EQ boost to add weight to bass and kick.** Distortion naturally adds midrange. A subtle LOW EQ boost helps the bass layers sit back and provides foundation.

**PUNK pairs beautifully with a VCF.** Feed PUNK into a low-pass filter for flexible tone shaping. A cutoff CV envelope lets you sweep from distorted harshness to distorted smoothness.

**Blend is a tone-shaping tool, not just a wet/dry knob.** Use small amounts of blend (10–40%) to add density and presence without obvious distortion. Use high blend (70–100%) for aggression.

**In a send/return setup, always set BLEND to 100% (fully wet).** The send/return already handles the dry/wet mix at the return fader. Setting BLEND to wet ensures maximum flexibility and no phase issues from the parallel clean signal.

**Experiment with both distortion stages at moderate levels.** Rather than cranking one gain all the way, try FET Gain ~60% + FUZZ Gain ~50%. The interaction between the two stages often sounds more musical and complex than stacking them at extremes.

**At sub-audio rates, PUNK can also distort LFOs and modulation sources.** Feed an LFO through PUNK for waveshaping. Patch PUNK OUT to a VCA CV input for interesting modulation coloration.
