# Modes

**PUNK** has no dedicated mode switches, but its character is shaped by which distortion stage you emphasize and the DIODE/MOSFET toggle on the fuzz circuit.

## Preamp Mode (FET Gain only)

Fuzz Gain all the way down, FET Gain up. The 80s cassette preamp saturates the signal with vintage warmth. Best for:

- Adding harmonic grit and fullness to drums and bass
- Fattening a lead synth without destroying its character
- Creating subtle distortion on ambient textures
- Tape-like saturation on drum buses

The preamp adds density and presence. It's the subtle, musical distortion.

## Fuzz Mode (Fuzz Gain only)

FET Gain down, Fuzz Gain up. The guitar pedal fuzz takes over. The DIODE/MOSFET toggle then controls the fuzz character:

**Fuzz (Diode)** — warm, singing, sustaining. The fuzz is vocal and round. Good for vintage, classic tones and leads.

**Fuzz (MOSFET)** — aggressive, brighter, faster. The fuzz has more attitude and dynamic response. Better for percussive sounds, drums, and "punk" attitude.

## Blended Mode (Both gains raised)

Raise both FET Gain and Fuzz Gain. The two distortion stages interact and stack, creating complex, multi-layered saturation. The DIODE/MOSFET toggle colors the fuzz layer underneath the preamp. This is where **PUNK** gets truly aggressive and interesting.

Common blended approach: FET Gain ~50%, Fuzz Gain ~70% (MOSFET mode) for a thick, gritty, yet still articulate distortion character.

## Baxandall EQ Shaping

The two-band EQ is independent of the distortion modes:

- **LOW EQ** — boost to add weight to bass-heavy signals, cut to reduce muddiness
- **HIGH EQ** — boost to add presence and bite (especially effective on fuzz), cut to tame harshness

The Baxandall design uses wide, gentle curves and is known for transparency and minimal phase shift. Use it subtly or boldly—it's musical either way.

## Clean Blend as an Effect

The BLEND knob is perhaps the most powerful control. At 0%, you get pure clean signal. At 100%, fully distorted. Anything in between is a mix of both. This means **PUNK** can function as:

- A **parallel compressor** (reduce distortion blend) to add thickness without losing dynamics
- A **tone shaper** (blend just enough distortion for presence)
- An **effect processor** (fully wet for aggression, fully dry for bypass)

Many players use a small amount of distortion blended in for tone shaping rather than dramatic overdrive.
