# Tips

## Start with Scatter for Percussion

The scatter noise type produces irregular spiky bursts that sit well under drum processing. Patch it through a fast-decaying envelope via EXT IN on another module, or run it through the dual filter with a narrow band for typewriter-style hits.

## Use LEDs as CV Meters

The LED indicators show the instantaneous CV sum applied to each section. In a patch with complex modulation routing, watch the LEDs to verify CV is arriving and at what intensity — particularly useful when troubleshooting quiet or unresponsive behavior.

## Crush CV for Rhythmic Destruction

Modulate the CRUSH CV with a clock-synced envelope or LFO to rhythmically toggle between clean and crushed states. At the attack the crusher is light; at the peak it digs in. This gives drum hits a dynamic grit envelope.

## Layer Noise Colors

Use two instances (or pair with another noise source) to layer different noise types before summing in a mixer. White and scatter together produce full-spectrum noise with organic pop variations on top.

## Pair with UTOPIA

**DYSTOPIA** and **UTOPIA** are designed as companion modules. Use UTOPIA's attenuverters to scale the filter CV inputs, the offset generator to set a base filter frequency, and the LFO to modulate CRUSH or LP/HP simultaneously.

## Random Gate Rate via Crush

While CRUSH doesn't directly control gate rate, crushing the digital noise signal changes its density and character. Lower crush rates produce slower, lower-frequency random gate events. This gives crude but effective rate control over the random gate without additional modules.
