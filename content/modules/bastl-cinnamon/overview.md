# Overview

**CINNAMON** is a 5 HP voltage-controlled state variable filter (SVF) from Bastl Instruments. The state variable topology provides simultaneous low-pass, band-pass, and high-pass outputs from the same audio input, all responding to the same cutoff frequency and resonance settings.

What distinguishes **CINNAMON** from a standard SVF is the addition of a drive switch (which overdrives the filter input for saturation effects) and two character switches that alter the internal feedback routing and change how resonance behaves. The character switches can produce markedly different filter tones and — when resonance is maxed out — different self-oscillation waveforms. They may also degrade the V/Oct tracking accuracy.

At maximum resonance the filter self-oscillates, generating a sine wave across all three outputs at 0°, 90°, and 180° phase relationships. This makes **CINNAMON** usable as a simple sine oscillator or a quadrature/phase-offset source.

## At a Glance

- **5 HP**, 35 mm deep
- **State variable filter**: simultaneous LP (2-pole), BP (1-pole), HP (2-pole)
- **Drive switch** — overdrives the filter input
- **2× Character switches** — alter resonance character and self-oscillation waveform
- **Self-oscillates** at maximum resonance — 0°/90°/180° sine outputs
- **V/Oct input** for pitch tracking
- **CV input** with attenuator
- **Power**: 30 mA (+12 V) / 30 mA (−12 V)
