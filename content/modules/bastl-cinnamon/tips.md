# Tips

## All Three Outputs at Once

The LP, BP, and HP outputs are always simultaneously active. Route them to separate channels in a mixer and blend — you can sculpt a custom filter response by combining them in different proportions. More LP = darker; more HP = brighter; more BP = hollow or vocal.

## Character Switches as Sound Design Tool

The character switches are not simply "on" or "off" improvements — they change the fundamental personality of the resonance. Try all four combinations (both off, 1 on, 2 on, both on) with resonance high and audio running, and pick the one that fits the sound. Some combinations are smoother; others are more aggressive or unstable.

## Drive Before Resonance

The DRIVE switch saturates the input before the filter core. Running a relatively quiet signal through DRIVE on produces subtle warmth. Running a hot signal (from a loud oscillator or boosted with a utility) through DRIVE creates hard saturation that then feeds into resonance — useful for aggressive, dirty sounds.

## Self-Oscillation as a Pitched Instrument

With V/OCT properly calibrated and RESONANCE at max, **CINNAMON** is a sine wave VCO. It is naturally pure-sounding (cleaner than most oscillators) and the 90° phase output from BP lets you create phasing/chorus effects using two outputs of the same pitch.

## V/Oct Accuracy and Character Switches

If you need accurate pitch tracking during self-oscillation (for melodic use), test your character switch combination with a tuner. Both switches off gives the most stable tracking. Some combinations drift at the extremes of the pitch range — acceptable for experimental use but not for in-tune melodic playing.

## LFO-Rate Self-Oscillation

Set FREQ low and RESONANCE at maximum — **CINNAMON** becomes a sine LFO. Use LP OUT as a smooth modulation source for filter cutoffs, VCA levels, or pitch. The sine is genuinely low-distortion at LFO rates, which is useful when you want clean undulation rather than the slightly distorted sine of many LFO modules.

## Quadrature Phasing

LP and BP in self-oscillation are 90° apart. Feed them into two inputs of a ring modulator with a fixed carrier — the phase difference creates a classic rotating-speaker or phaser effect. Or use them to drive a stereo VCA in opposite polarities for auto-pan.

## Stacking with Another Filter

Because **CINNAMON** produces all three responses simultaneously, you can use one output for tone shaping (LP to another filter in series) and another for effect (HP to a reverb send) from the same patch point, without splitting the signal.
