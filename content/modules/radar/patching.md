# Patch Recipes

## Acoustic Drum Trigger

Attach the contact mic to a drum shell or cymbal. Set SENSITIVITY to suit the drum's volume level. Patch GATE OUT to a sequencer reset, envelope trigger, or drum module trigger. Each drum hit advances or triggers the patch in sync with the physical performance.

## Envelope-Following Filter

Patch AUDIO OUT to a VCF audio input. Patch ENV OUT to the same VCF's cutoff CV input. Each tap or stroke on the contact surface opens the filter in sync with the hit's amplitude — the harder you play, the brighter the filter. DECAY switch controls how long the filter stays open.

## Physical Performance to Modulation

Attach the contact mic to any resonant surface — guitar body, wooden table, metal frame. Patch ENV OUT to a VCA or reverb mix. Tap patterns on the surface; the envelope output follows your performance dynamics, modulating the patch in response.

## Acoustic Trigger for Generative Patch

Patch GATE OUT to the Chaos T section clock or an external clock divider. Physical percussion advances a generative sequence — the tempo is determined by how fast you tap. Combine with ENV OUT to modulation to link performance dynamics to the generated melody.

## Layered Acoustic + Synthesised Sound

Patch AUDIO OUT through a VCF and effects to process the raw contact mic sound. Simultaneously patch ENV OUT and GATE OUT to a separate synthesised voice (envelope + trigger). Each physical hit produces both the processed acoustic sound and a triggered synthesiser note — a layered acoustic/electronic texture.

## Contact Mic on a Synth Chassis

Attach the contact mic to the outside panel of your Eurorack case. Physical knob-turning vibrations and patching sounds become subtle modulation. Extremely low-level signals — GAIN needs to be near maximum and SENSITIVITY on high. Creates a meta-feedback between physical interaction and the patch's modulation.
