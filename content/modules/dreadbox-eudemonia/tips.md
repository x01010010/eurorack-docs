# Tips

## Watch the Peak LED

The VCA peak LED fires above 10 VPTP. If it's constantly lit, reduce the mixer input levels or lower the GAIN knob. Brief flashes on transients are acceptable — sustained illumination indicates clipping in the output stage.

## Use LEDs to Verify CV Routing

Each LED shows the real-time CV sum for its section. If a filter or VCA isn't responding as expected, the LED tells you whether CV is arriving and at what intensity. A dim or unmoving LED means the CV source isn't reaching the section.

## Set HP to Taste, Not to Zero

The HP filter is easy to overlook but powerful for mix placement. A slight HP cutoff (just trimming rumble and low-end mud) makes the final output sit higher in a mix without removing warmth. Try setting HP at around 80–120 Hz as a default starting point.

## Series Filters for Mid-Scoop

Set LP CUTOFF to around 2 kHz and HP CUTOFF to around 500 Hz. This creates a band-pass that emphasises the upper-mids while cutting both bass and high frequencies — useful for lo-fi telephone or radio effects, or for narrowing a sound to sit in a specific frequency range.

## Pair with UTOPIA for Full Voice Processing

Use **UTOPIA**'s attenuverters to scale CV before it reaches **EUDEMONIA**'s filter and VCA inputs. The offset generator adds a base voltage to keep the VCA open at a minimum level while an envelope adds expression on top. The two modules together handle the full mix-filter-VCA chain with precise CV management.
