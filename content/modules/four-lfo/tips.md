# Tips & Tricks

- **Quadrature mode for circular modulation** — patching the 0°/90°/180°/270° outputs to four panning or pitch targets creates smooth rotary motion that never repeats exactly when rates are slightly different.
- **RESET is powerful for live performance** — patch a button-triggered gate to all RESET inputs. Pressing the button syncs all four LFOs to the same start point, useful for re-locking modulation after a long performance drift.
- **Divide mode as a free clock divider** — the SQR output of a channel in Divide mode is a gate signal at an integer division of channel 1. Use it as a rhythmic trigger without needing a dedicated clock divider module.
- **Very slow rates need patience** — at the bottom of the fader range, LFO periods can exceed many minutes. Useful for slow macro-structure changes in ambient music but easy to forget you've set it that low.
- **ASN waveform choice matters for destinations** — use triangle/sine for smooth modulation of pitch or filter; use sawtooth for rhythmic ramp effects; use stepped random on volume or pan for generative variety.
- **Mix SIN outputs for complex shapes** — patch two or three SIN channels at different rates to a unity mixer. The summed output is a complex wave that slowly drifts phase. Route to a single destination for rich, unpredictable modulation.
