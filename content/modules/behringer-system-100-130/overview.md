# Overview

**130 DUAL VCA** is a 16 HP analog dual voltage-controlled amplifier based on the Roland System 100M 130 module from the early 1980s. Each of the two independent VCA channels is a small mixer in its own right — three signal inputs with individual gain knobs feed into a single VCA core, with three modulation inputs controlling the gain level via CV.

Each channel offers switchable linear or exponential response and two output levels: a high-impedance LOW output suitable for instrument-level destinations and a low-impedance HIGH output for line-level gear. This makes the 130 flexible enough to interface between Eurorack and external equipment.

## Sonic Territory

- **Envelope-controlled dynamics** — the classic use: patch an envelope into a MOD IN and shape amplitude over time.
- **VCA mixing** — three SIG IN jacks per channel let you mix audio sources before they hit the VCA, reducing module count.
- **AM/tremolo** — patch an LFO into MOD IN for amplitude modulation effects.
- **CV-controlled gain staging** — use steady CV to set precise gain levels for signal routing and mixing duties.
- **Ring modulation-like textures** — audio-rate CV into MOD IN produces AM sidebands.

## At a Glance

- **16 HP** Eurorack module
- **Two independent VCA channels**
- **3 signal inputs** per channel with individual gain knobs
- **3 modulation inputs** per channel with individual level knobs
- **INITIAL GAIN** knob sets base gain without CV
- **LINEAR / EXPONENTIAL** response switch per channel
- **HIGH and LOW outputs** per channel (line and instrument level)
- **Signal and clip LEDs** per channel
- **Power**: 30 mA (+12 V) / 20 mA (-12 V)
