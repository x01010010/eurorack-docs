# Panel & I/O

## Top section — transport & display

The top section houses the screen, transport controls (PLAY, STOP, REC), tempo encoder, and the main mode/menu controls. Pattern, song, and edit modes are reached from here.

## Step buttons

The classic 16 lit step buttons run along the bottom. Selected steps are bright; unselected steps are dim. With **SHIFT** held, the step buttons gain secondary functions (length, scale, micro-edits).

## Track selectors

Ten track-select buttons let you switch which track is being edited. Each track has its own length, swing, probability, and step pattern.

## Outputs

| Jack | Type | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| TRIG OUT 1–10 | 3.5 mm TS, DC coupled | One per track |
| ACCENT OUT 1–10 | 3.5 mm TS, DC coupled | One per track, fires when a step is marked accented |
| CV OUT | 3.5 mm TS, DC coupled | Per-track pitch CV (selectable) |
| GATE OUT | 3.5 mm TS, DC coupled | Companion gate to CV OUT |
| CLK OUT | 3.5 mm TS, DC coupled | Master clock output, follows internal tempo |

## Inputs

| Jack | Type | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CLK IN | 3.5 mm TS, DC coupled | External clock input |
| RESET / GATE IN | 3.5 mm TS, DC coupled | Reset the sequence, or use as a transport gate |

## Digital connectivity

| Connector | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| MIDI IN | 5-pin DIN |
| MIDI OUT | 5-pin DIN |
| USB-C | USB-MIDI host/device, firmware via SynthTribe |

The 22 patch outputs and 2 patch inputs make this the most jack-rich Behringer sequencer in Eurorack format.
