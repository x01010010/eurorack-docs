# Tips

## Attenuverter Center as a Kill Switch

Turn an attenuverter to center (fully CCW from noon is mute) to instantly silence a CV or audio path in a live set. Use two channels — one active, one muted — to A/B between modulation sources by swapping knob positions.

## Offset as Manual CV

With no input patched to an attenuverter, patch the offset output directly to a parameter that needs a static voltage (e.g., VCO tune, filter cutoff). The offset knob becomes a manual CV control.

## Use the Mixer to Sum Envelopes

Route two different envelopes into the mixer to create compound contours — a fast attack from one, a slow decay from another. Scale each with an attenuverter before the mix for balance.

## Pulserizer for Drum Extraction

Patch a mixed audio signal (e.g., from a feedback patch) into the Pulserizer. As the mix gets louder, the Pulserizer output fires gates. These gates can retrigger envelopes or advance a sequencer, creating rhythmic feedback loops.

## LFO at Audio Rate for Suboctave

Tune the LFO to the pitch of a playing VCO (440 Hz = A4). The LFO output will be a sine-like wave at the same pitch, which you can pass through the Pulserizer to get a square suboctave or blend into the audio path for harmonic thickening.
