# Tips & Tricks

- **CYCLE + short RISE + long FALL = descending ramp LFO** — the asymmetric time settings make a triangle LFO into a sawtooth-like wave, useful for pitch vibrato with a slow droop.
- **Use OR as a logical merge** — if two sequencer tracks have complementary patterns, OR combines their triggers into one dense rhythm without needing a separate logic module.
- **INV for push-pull modulation** — patch CH2 OUT to a VCA and INV to a VCF; as the VCA closes the filter opens, creating a classic ducking filter effect from a single envelope.
- **CV over RISE/FALL extends range dramatically** — even moderate CV voltages can push rise/fall times far beyond what the knob alone covers. Patch an envelope to RISE CV to create an envelope-controlled slew.
- **EXP curve for natural envelopes** — logarithmic hearing means exponential voltage curves sound linear. Use EXP for envelopes that should sound smooth and natural (strings, pads). Use LIN for mechanical, robotic character.
- **Four attenuverters as a mixer** — patch four different signals to ATT 1–4 and then manually mix them downstream; the attenuverter knobs act as four mixer faders.
