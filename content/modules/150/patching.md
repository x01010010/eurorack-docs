# Patch Recipes

## Classic Random CV Source

Patch WHITE 1 to S&H IN. Patch a clock (from a sequencer or LFO SQUARE OUT) to TRIG IN. S&H OUT delivers a new random voltage on each clock pulse — patch to VCO pitch, filter cutoff, or pan for stepped random modulation.

## Ring Mod Bell Tones

Patch a sine wave VCO into X IN and a second VCO (slightly detuned or at a harmonic interval) into Y IN. RING MOD OUT produces bell-like inharmonic tones. Tune the two oscillators to musical intervals (perfect 5th, octave) for pitched metallic tones, or use dissonant intervals for gongs and tam-tam sounds.

## AM Tremolo

Patch an audio VCO into Y IN. Patch the LFO SINE OUT to X IN. RING MOD OUT produces amplitude modulation tremolo. Increase LFO rate into audio range for FM/AM sideband effects.

## Noise as Audio Source

Patch WHITE 1 or PINK 1 directly to a VCF audio input. Use the filter to shape the noise into drums (closed hi-hat, snare body) or wind/breath textures. White = bright; pink = warmer.

## Multi-LFO from One Module

Use all five LFO waveform outputs simultaneously: SINE to a VCF, SQUARE to a tremolo VCA, TRIANGLE to pitch vibrato, SAW to a rhythmic pan sweep, RAMP to reverb mix automation. All five at once from a single RATE knob.

## Noise-Clocked S&H Drone

Patch PINK 2 to S&H IN. Patch the LFO SQUARE at a slow rate to TRIG IN. The S&H holds pink noise values for regular durations, creating a stepped, slowly-wandering CV. Route to a long-release drone VCO for drifting pitch.
