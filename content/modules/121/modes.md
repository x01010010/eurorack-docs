# Modes

## Low-Pass Mode (LP switch position)

The filter attenuates frequencies above the cutoff at 24 dB/octave. At low resonance, it produces a smooth, warm sound. Increasing resonance emphasises the cutoff frequency. At maximum resonance the filter self-oscillates, producing a sine-wave tone at the cutoff frequency — usable as a pure sine VCO.

## High-Pass Mode (HP switch position)

The filter attenuates frequencies below the cutoff at 24 dB/octave. Useful for removing low-frequency rumble, thinning sounds, or isolating high-frequency content. Resonance in HP mode emphasises a peak at the cutoff in the high-frequency range.

## Self-Oscillation

With RESONANCE fully clockwise in LP mode, the filter oscillates. The pitch of oscillation tracks V/OCT at 1V/octave. This turns the 121 into a dual sine-wave oscillator — useful for testing, generating pure tones, or FM/PM patching.

## Parallel vs Serial Filtering

Both filter channels are independent. Use them in parallel (same source into both, mixed at output) for wider, more complex filtering. Wire them in series (OUT of filter 1 → SIG input of filter 2) for a combined 48 dB/oct slope or for sequential HP + LP bandpass approximation.
