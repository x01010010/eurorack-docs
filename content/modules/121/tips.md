# Tips & Tricks

- **Three signal inputs = built-in mixer** — use SIG 1, 2, and 3 to mix multiple sources before filtering. The level knobs act as a 3-channel pre-filter mixer, saving a separate utility mixer.
- **Three mod inputs for layered CV** — patch an envelope to MOD 1, an LFO to MOD 2, and a sequencer to MOD 3. The filter cutoff follows all three simultaneously, creating complex, time-varying filter motion.
- **48 dB/oct with series connection** — connect both filters in series, both in LP mode, and tune both cutoffs together. The combined slope is 48 dB/oct — an extremely steep, tight filter useful for sharp bassline cuts.
- **Self-oscillating sine VCO** — at maximum resonance the 121 oscillates cleanly in LP mode. It V/OCT-tracks accurately enough to play melodies. Useful as an emergency oscillator or for pure sine waves without a dedicated sine VCO.
- **HP mode for drum processing** — set a filter to HP with cutoff around 100–300 Hz. Route a drum bus through it to remove kick rumble from the mix, tightening the low end without affecting the body of the drums.
- **Slight detuning between filters for stereo width** — when using both filters on a stereo source, offset one CUTOFF knob by a few percent. The slight difference in frequency response creates a subtle stereo width enhancement.
