# Tips & Tricks

- **Vactrol variation is a feature** — vactrols have inherent component-to-component variation. Each Black LPG will respond slightly differently to the same trigger, lending organic unpredictability to percussion patches.
- **Stack the EG OUT** — the envelope output isn't just for external use. Mult it back into the CV input (through an attenuverter) for pseudo-exponential decay curves, or patch it to other modules for synchronized modulation.
- **Overdrive at low resonance** — the overdrive switch still adds harmonic content even at moderate resonance settings. It doesn't require full self-oscillation to be useful — try it at 10–12 o'clock for subtle warmth.
- **Gate length doesn't matter** — the decay envelope always completes its full cycle. Short triggers and long gates produce the same response. This means clock divisions and gate sequencers work identically to trigger sources.
- **Use MODE CV for per-step variation** — sequence the mode CV to alternate between VCF and VCF+VCA on different steps. This creates a pattern where some notes sustain (VCF-only, amplitude unaffected) while others pluck (VCF+VCA shapes both).
- **Pair with a mixer for parallel processing** — mult your audio and send one copy dry, one through Black LPG. Blend to taste for parallel compression-like effects where the plucked signal adds transient detail.
- **Short decay as a waveshaper** — with very short decay times and audio-rate triggers, the envelope modulation interacts with the signal in waveshaper-like ways, adding sidebands and grit.
