# Tips & Tricks

## The Default Gain of 0.5 Is Intentional

The factory max gain of ~0.5 (−6 dB) exists to prevent the summed output from clipping when all four channels run at full amplitude. Don't be tempted to immediately modify the resistors — instead, treat the gain budget as a feature. You have headroom to bring multiple channels up simultaneously without distortion.

## Use the CV Attenuators for Fine Balancing

The CV attenuators are not just for scaling hot CV sources — they are also a way to fine-balance four channels against each other when you want different voices to sit at different relative levels under the same envelope or gate. Set the level knobs equal and use the attenuators to stagger the effective gain.

## Mix CVs, Not Just Audio

Because the module is DC coupled, it is a very capable modulation combiner. Sum an envelope, an LFO, and a sequencer CV through three channels, scale each with the level knob, and the output is a composite modulation signal. This is useful for adding subtle rhythmic texture to a pitch CV or creating complex gate envelopes.

## Chain Two Units for an 8-Channel Stereo Mixer

Two A-135-3 units in master/slave configuration give 8 independent stereo channels with individual CV control in just 16 HP — a very dense and cost-effective mixing solution.

## Watch Out for the Knob-less Attenuators

The CV attenuators have no knobs — only plastic shafts with a white dot. In a dark performance environment they can be easy to accidentally bump. Mark each shaft position with a paint pen after dialing in a patch you want to preserve.
