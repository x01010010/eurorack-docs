# Modes

**SG** has no mode switches — all four divisions are always active and mixed according to the four knob positions. The "mode" of the module is defined by which knobs you turn up and by what waveform you feed into the input.

## Sub-Harmonic Blending

The four knobs act as individual level controls for four distinct intervals below the input pitch:

**Knob 1 (7th below)** — produces a sub-harmonic a minor seventh below the input note. This is the most harmonically distant of the four layers; a little goes a long way. At low levels it adds subtle warmth; at higher levels it creates an unusual, slightly dissonant bass weight.

**Knob 2 (7th + 1 octave below)** — produces the same seventh interval but one full octave further down. This is the deepest and most unusual division. Most useful as a flavoring at low mix levels rather than a dominant layer.

**Knob 3 (1 octave below)** — produces a clean octave below the input. This is the most musically straightforward division: transparent, solid, and compatible with any tonality. The workhorse knob for adding bass weight.

**Knob 4 (2 octaves below)** — produces a pitch two full octaves below the input. At audio rate this sits in the sub-bass register. Very effective for adding sub-bass extension to a VCO that doesn't naturally go that low.

## Using SG as a Clock Sub-Divider

At sub-audio rates, **SG** divides any incoming clock or LFO signal. Feed a clock pulse into the input and the four outputs (reflected in the mix knob positions) produce sub-divided rhythmic pulses at ÷2 and ÷4 rates, with the 7th-interval divisions producing non-standard rhythmic subdivisions. This is a secondary use, not the primary design intent, but fully functional.
