# Tips & Tricks

- **User slots are the real value** — the FV-1 has a large library of free community programs. Uploading custom programs transforms the module into a completely different effect. Keep a set of programs saved to re-upload as needed.
- **32 kHz sample rate colours the sound** — the FV-1 runs at 32 kHz, which gives reverbs and delays a slightly dark, lo-fi character. This is often an asset rather than a flaw — it sits well in dense mixes and adds warmth to digital reverb tails.
- **CV inputs respond to slow modulation** — fast LFOs into the P CV inputs can cause zipper noise in some programs. Slow envelopes, gentle LFOs, and sequencer CVs are more reliable. If using fast CV, attenuate first.
- **IN R normalling means mono works fine** — there is no need to patch both inputs for a mono source. One cable into IN L is sufficient; the DSP processes in stereo and the outputs are always stereo regardless.
- **Set DRY/WET fully wet for send/return use** — when using the module as a reverb send, fully wet output lets you control the blend at the mixing stage. A partial DRY/WET in the module while also blending at the mixer introduces phase issues.
- **Factory program order varies** — different firmware versions may have different programs in the factory slots. When documenting or sharing patches, refer to program names or types rather than slot numbers.
