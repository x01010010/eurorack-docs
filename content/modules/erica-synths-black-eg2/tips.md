# Tips & Tricks

- **Gate length as a performance tool** — turn the GATE LENGTH knob live during a sequence to morph between staccato triggers and sustained notes without changing your sequencer programming.
- **Loop mode tempo sync** — in loop mode, patch a clock into GATE IN to reset the cycle on each pulse. This syncs the complex LFO waveshape to your tempo while the ADSR knobs control the shape within each period.
- **Zero sustain for AD envelopes** — set SUSTAIN fully counter-clockwise and the envelope becomes a simple attack-decay shape regardless of gate length. Useful for percussive patches where you don't want a sustain plateau.
- **Maximum sustain for ASR** — set SUSTAIN fully clockwise and DECAY to minimum. The envelope effectively becomes an attack-sustain-release shape — instant peak hold until gate drops.
- **Stacking both channels** — patch both outputs to the same destination through a mixer or OR combiner. Use different ADSR times on each channel for complex, multi-peak envelope shapes from a single trigger.
- **Gate button for sound design** — use the manual GATE button to audition envelope shapes when designing a patch. Hold it to hear the full ADSR cycle; tap it quickly to hear how the gate length circuit extends your trigger.
- **CV gate length from an envelope** — patch the output of channel 1 into the GL CV of channel 2. The first envelope's shape dynamically controls how long the second envelope sustains — creating interdependent, evolving modulation.
