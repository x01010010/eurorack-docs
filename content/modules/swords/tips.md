# Tips & Tricks

## SHIFT is the headline feature

Most dual filters force you to choose between two independent voices and a tightly-coupled stereo pair. SHIFT gives you both: the cutoffs are locked together (so they sweep as one) but offset by an amount you set. Engage SHIFT, set filter 2 FREQ to ~1 o'clock, and you have a stereo filter pair with a constant musical interval between channels.

## Use as a stereo sine VCO

Crank both RES knobs to maximum. Patch V/oct into both V/OCT jacks. Take the two per-filter outputs as L and R. You now have two sine VCOs that track 1 V/oct, panned stereo. Detune slightly with the FREQ knobs for a gorgeous stereo pad.

## Wavefolder character

The wavefolder isn't a separate stage — it's a region of the DRIVE / RESPONSE controls. Push DRIVE up and RESPONSE clockwise; at the extremes you get aggressive folded harmonics. Excellent on simple sine or triangle waves where the folding generates the upper harmonics that the filter then sculpts.

## Attenuverters are mandatory

The bipolar attenuverters on FREQ, RES, and ROUTING CV inputs are not optional. Most modulation sources (envelopes, LFOs) will overload the input range without them. Start with the attenuverter at 12 o'clock (zero) and dial up.

## Routing CV for evolving topology

Patch a slow LFO into ROUTING CV with a small attenuverter setting. The routing topology drifts through single / parallel / series over time, creating a constantly-evolving stereo image. Strange, distinctive, hard to do anywhere else.

## Ping for percussion

With high resonance and short triggers into SIG IN, the filter "pings" at the cutoff frequency — a percussive bell-like tone. Use a 140 envelope with very short A and D into SIG IN (via VCA), modulate FREQ for melodic ping melodies.

## Pair with WAVES for stereo wavefolded pads

Patch WAVES UNI OUT into SIG IN 1 and WAVES BI OUT into SIG IN 2. Set both filters to band-pass with high resonance, SHIFT engaged. The two filtered wavefolded streams of WAVES become a wide stereo pad with constant motion.

## Mono compatibility

When using stereo mode with the two per-filter outputs, the two outputs sum cleanly to mono. No phase issues. Safe for radio play, club PA, or mono summing busses.

## Same chip as Blades

The V2164 chip is identical to the one in the original Blades. The PCB layout differs and the panel is more compact, but the audio character is essentially the original — at a fraction of the price.
