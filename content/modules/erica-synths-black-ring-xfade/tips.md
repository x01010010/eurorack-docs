# Tips

## The Carrier Level Knob Is the Main Sound Design Control

Unlike a simple on/off ring modulator, the **BLACK RING-XFADE** rewards careful attention to carrier level. The waveshaping zone at partial carrier (roughly 10–50% level) produces tones not available from either a clean signal or full ring modulation. Automate the carrier level with a slow LFO or envelope to continuously morph timbre.

## Use Two Octave-Related Oscillators for Musical Ring Mod

Fully inharmonic ring modulation (random interval between signal and carrier) gets harsh quickly. For musical use, tune the carrier to an interval relationship with the signal: octave, fifth, third. The sum and difference frequencies then fall on musically related pitches rather than clashing partials.

## AC Coupling for Audio Crossfades, DC for CV Morphing

The AC/DC switch on the crossfader is not decorative. Running CV through the AC-coupled mode will distort or lose the signal's DC offset — set it to DC whenever crossfading between CVs, envelopes, or any signal where offset matters.

## Positive VCA Bias for Tremolo and AM

With positive bias on the VCA and an LFO patched to VCA CV, the output trembles around a non-zero level — classic tremolo or amplitude modulation. Without the bias, the LFO dips the VCA below zero and you lose the negative half of the modulation depth, reducing the AM effect.

## Break the Normalizations for Complex Routing

The three normalizations (RM1→XF-A, RM2→XF-B, XF→VCA) make common signal chains convenient, but patching into XFADE IN A, XFADE IN B, or VCA IN with external cables opens the module to much more flexible use. Example: use the VCA as a standalone envelope VCA feeding the crossfader with an external mix, while both ring modulators are used elsewhere.

## Waveshaping Without Ring Modulation

The partial-carrier waveshaping mode works well with an LFO at sub-audio rate on the carrier input. The filter-like sweeping effect is different from both a VCF and a conventional waveshaper. Try a triangle or sine LFO at a few Hz as the carrier with CARRIER LEVEL at 30–70% for a smooth morphing effect that adds and subtracts harmonic content rhythmically.

## Ring Modulating CVs

With the crossfader in DC coupling mode, both ring modulators can process CVs (envelopes, LFOs, pitch CVs). Ring-modulating a pitch CV with a slow LFO produces a vibrato-like pitch multiplication rather than straight pitch modulation — the output contains sum and difference of two changing CVs.
